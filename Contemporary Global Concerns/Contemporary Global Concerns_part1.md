### ğŸ¯ Core Theme

### ğŸ”‘ Key Recall Terms

### ğŸ“ Answer Structure

**Introduction:**
- Global conventions establish legal and cooperative framework
- Address transnational terrorism threat
- Efficacy remains mixed
- Depends on political will, enforcement mechanisms, international cooperation complexities

**Body:**

**Achievements - Legal Framework:**
- Defines and criminalizes terrorism
- International Convention for Suppression of Terrorism Financing (1999)
- International Convention for Suppression of Terrorist Bombings (1997)
- Common understanding of terrorist acts
- Targets terrorist networks' financial structures
- Obliges cooperation in investigation and prosecution
- UNOCT coordinates efforts and promotes compliance

**Achievements - Multilateral Cooperation:**
- Enables countries to work together
- Intelligence sharing, capacity building, law enforcement
- UN Global Counter-Terrorism Strategy (2006)
- Strengthens border controls and information exchange
- Enhances counter-terrorism capabilities
- Post-9/11: international cooperation dismantled Al-Qaeda financial networks

**Limitations - Definition Problem:**
- Lack of universally accepted definition
- Countries define terrorism based on national interests
- Complicates implementation of agreements
- Louise Richardson: "What Terrorists Want" - without common understanding, difficult to achieve consensus
- States categorize groups differently based on geopolitical priorities

**Limitations - Sovereignty Concerns:**
- Political and legal sovereignty limits effectiveness
- Countries hesitate to implement laws conflicting with domestic policies
- Compromise sovereignty concerns
- Uneven ratification and enforcement
- Countries prioritize national security over international commitments

**Limitations - Enforcement Mechanisms:**
- Lack of enforcement undermines efficacy
- UNSC Resolution 1373 mandates prevention of terrorism financing
- No global authorities with uniform enforcement power
- Leads to inconsistent implementation

**Conclusion:**
- Established crucial legal and cooperative framework
- Hindered by: no universal definition, sovereignty concerns, weak enforcement
- Need stronger commitment to multilateralism
- Standardized terrorism definition required
- Improved enforcement capabilities essential
- Ensure consistent and coordinated global action

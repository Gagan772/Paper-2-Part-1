## Q1. Account for the rise of European Union as a highly influential regional organisation. (2023) (15 Marks)

### ğŸ¯ Core Theme
The EU emerged as a highly influential regional organization through decades of economic integration, political cooperation, and strategic soft power projection. From the ECSC (1951) to the Maastricht Treaty (1992), the EU created the world's largest single market, established supranational governance structures, and projected normative power globally through democracy, human rights, and multilateralism.

### ğŸ”‘ Key Recall Terms
`European Coal and Steel Community` | `Treaty of Rome 1957` | `Maastricht Treaty 1992` | `Single Market` | `Euro Currency` | `Supranational Governance` | `Common Foreign and Security Policy` | `Normative Power Europe` | `Climate Leadership` | `Brexit Challenges`

### ğŸ“ Answer Structure

**Introduction:**
- EU formed from European Coal and Steel Community (ECSC) in 1951
- Evolved through Treaty of Rome (1957) and Maastricht Treaty (1992)
- Designed to promote economic cooperation and prevent conflicts in Europe
- Robert Schuman's vision: make war "not merely unthinkable but materially impossible"
- Created through economic integration and mutual dependence

**Economic Power:**
- Single market as one of the largest in the world
- 27 member states representing over 14% of global GDP
- Regulatory frameworks and trade policies have global implications
- Influence worldwide standards (Andrew Moravcsik)
- Euro introduced in 1999, used by 20 member states
- Solidified economic integration in global financial markets
- Ability to negotiate as single bloc in international trade agreements
- Critical player in World Trade Organization (WTO)

**Political Influence:**
- Unique supranational structure through European Parliament and Commission
- Member states collaborate on policies: environment to human rights
- Common Foreign and Security Policy (CFSP) framework
- United stance on international issues (sanctions against Russia, Middle East diplomacy)
- Ian Manners' concept of "normative power Europe"
- Projects values: democracy, human rights, rule of law globally
- Uses soft power to promote stability and development

**Diplomatic Weight:**
- Mediated Iran nuclear deal (JCPOA) in 2015
- Leadership in climate action through Paris Agreement
- Aims for carbon neutrality by 2050
- Shapes regional and global dynamics

**Challenges:**
- Political divisions highlighted by Brexit
- Disagreements over migration policies
- Internal tensions despite achievements

**Conclusion:**
- EU continues to shape regional and global dynamics
- Leverages economic power, political unity, and multilateralism commitment
- Remains influential force in international affairs despite challenges

- European Green Deal aims for carbon neutrality by 2050

- Eastern Europe countries more dependent on Russian energy
- Hungary expressed reluctance to fully support EU sanctions
- Divided stance within EU complicating unified action
- Different national interests creating policy challenges
- Geopolitical complexity in forming cohesive response

**Conclusion:**
- Crisis underscored EU's energy vulnerability
- Exposed geopolitical and economic risks of energy dependency
- Need for rapid but complex transition to secure, sustainable sources
- Achieving energy security requires immediate and long-term strategies
- EU faces balancing act between security and climate goals

- Showcases capability to address humanitarian crises collectively

- South China Sea disputes continue to test unity
- Differing political systems among members
- Myanmar situation creates internal tensions
- Effectiveness tested by complex geopolitical issues

- Provides model for regional cooperation and stability
- Despite challenges, maintains relevance and unity

## Q6. Since its inception the South Asian Association for Regional Cooperation (SAARC) has failed to deliver on its promise. What initiatives should be taken to reinvigorate the organisation? (2018) (15 Marks)

### ğŸ¯ Core Theme
SAARC (est. 1985) failed due to India-Pakistan rivalry paralyzing decision-making (2016 summit cancelled), weak institutional framework lacking enforcement authority, and minimal economic integration (<5% intra-regional trade vs ASEAN's 25%). Reinvigoration requires depoliticizing through conflict resolution mechanisms, deepening SAFTA for customs union, promoting infrastructure connectivity, and empowering the Secretariat with monitoring bodies and civil society involvement for implementation effectiveness.

### ğŸ”‘ Key Recall Terms
`SAARC 1985` | `India-Pakistan Rivalry` | `2016 Summit Cancellation` | `<5% Intra-Regional Trade` | `SAFTA Agreement` | `Weak Institutional Framework` | `Depoliticization Strategy` | `Conflict Resolution Mechanisms` | `Infrastructure Connectivity` | `Civil Society Involvement`

### ğŸ“ Answer Structure

**Introduction:**
- SAARC established in 1985
- Vision: regional integration, cooperation, development
- Member states: vast population and economic potential
- Struggled to deliver on promises
- Political conflicts, particularly India-Pakistan
- Economic disparities among members
- Structural limitations within organization
- C. Raja Mohan: inability to overcome geopolitical tensions
- India-Pakistan rivalry paralyzed decision-making and hindered progress

**Limited Mandate and Weak Framework:**
- Unlike EU or ASEAN, SAARC lacks enforcement authority
- Cannot enforce agreements or resolve conflicts
- Often symbolic rather than substantive presence
- 19th SAARC summit (2016) postponed due to India-Pakistan tensions
- Bilateral issues disrupt multilateral cooperation
- Economic integration remains low
- Intra-regional trade <5% of total trade
- Significantly lower than ASEAN's ~25%

**Initiative 1 - Depoliticization:**
- Shift towards depoliticizing organization
- Create mechanisms prioritizing economic and development cooperation
- Separate from political disputes
- Establish conflict resolution frameworks (like ASEAN Regional Forum)
- Mitigate tensions, focus on shared economic/social goals
- Employ neutral entities or third-party mediators
- Address India-Pakistan conflicts separately
- Allow rest of region to progress on cooperation

**Initiative 2 - Economic Integration:**
- Deepen economic integration
- Enhance South Asian Free Trade Area (SAFTA) agreement
- Create customs union
- Promote cross-border investments
- Stimulate trade and economic growth
- Promote infrastructure projects: energy and transport corridors
- Strengthen physical and economic connectivity
- Similar to Belt and Road Initiative but intra-regional focus

**Initiative 3 - Institutional Strengthening:**
- Empower SAARC institutions (especially Secretariat)

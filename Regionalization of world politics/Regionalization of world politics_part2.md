## Q11. Despite so many agreed areas of cooperation, innumerable institutional mechanisms and a permanent secretariat, South Asian Association for Regional Cooperation (SAARC) has not taken off as a meaningful regional grouping. Discuss. (2014) (20 Marks)

### ğŸ¯ Core Theme
SAARC (founded 1985) failed to become effective despite permanent secretariat in Kathmandu and specialized bodies because India-Pakistan rivalry (Kashmir, 2016 summit cancellation) paralyzes unanimity-based decision-making, economic asymmetries create power imbalances, minimal intra-regional trade (<5% vs ASEAN's 25%) stems from SAFTA's ineffective implementation with Pakistan denying India MFN status, and weak institutional structure lacks enforcement mechanismsâ€”requiring restructuring toward economic prioritization over bilateral disputes for meaningful regional cooperation.

### ğŸ”‘ Key Recall Terms
`SAARC 1985` | `India-Pakistan Kashmir` | `2016 Summit Cancelled` | `<5% Intra-Trade` | `SAFTA Ineffective` | `Unanimity Deadlock` | `Kathmandu Secretariat` | `Power Asymmetry` | `MFN Status Denial` | `ASEAN Comparison`

### ğŸ“ Answer Structure

**Introduction:**
- SAARC founded 1985
- Member countries: Afghanistan, Bangladesh, Bhutan, India, Maldives, Nepal, Pakistan, Sri Lanka
- Aim: promote economic and regional integration
- Despite numerous cooperation areas, institutional mechanisms, permanent secretariat
- Struggled to become meaningful and effective regional grouping
- Core reasons: geopolitical tensions (India-Pakistan), structural/economic asymmetries
- Hindered genuine cooperation and integration

**India-Pakistan Rivalry as Major Obstacle:**
- Ongoing conflicts, particularly over Kashmir
- Frequent border tensions between two nuclear-armed states
- Often overshadowed SAARC's agenda
- Paralyzed decision-making processes
- S. D. Muni (South Asian scholar): until disputes resolved or insulated from SAARC, organization will struggle
- 19th SAARC Summit scheduled in Islamabad (2016) cancelled
- India cited security concerns and cross-border terrorism
- Decided to boycott event
- Led to other members pulling out
- Demonstrates how bilateral issues derail regional cooperation

**Power and Economic Asymmetry:**
- India as largest and most economically powerful member
- Dominates the region
- Leads to perceptions of hegemonic behavior by smaller neighbors
- Nepal and Sri Lanka expressed concerns over India's influence
- Fear interests may be overshadowed
- C. Raja Mohan (expert on South Asian politics): imbalance makes equal partnership difficult
- Smaller states seek to counterbalance India's influence
- Align with external powers like China
- Further complicates regional dynamics

**Lack of Intra-Regional Trade:**
- South Asia remains least economically integrated region globally
- World Bank: intra-regional trade <5% of total trade
- Compared to ASEAN's over 25%
- SAARC established South Asian Free Trade Area (SAFTA) for economic cooperation
- Tariff and non-tariff barriers persist
- Political tensions prevented effective implementation
- Pakistan's reluctance to grant Most Favored Nation (MFN) status to India
- Impeded trade normalization

**Institutional Weaknesses:**
- Permanent secretariat in Kathmandu exists
- Various specialized bodies address poverty, health, education
- Decision-making process requires unanimity
- Often led to deadlocks
- Structure doesn't allow flexibility
- Cannot bypass political disputes to implement regional programs
- Kathleen McNamara: regional organizations need strong institutional mechanisms
- Must enforce agreements and ensure cooperation beyond political disagreements
- SAARC lacks this capacity

**Contrast with ASEAN:**
- Other regional groupings like ASEAN achieved integration despite diversity
- ASEAN's success attributed to economic cooperation focus
- Ability to resolve disputes through consensus and non-interference
- SAARC unable to separate regional cooperation from bilateral disputes
- Resulted in stagnation

**Conclusion:**
- SAARC established numerous mechanisms and frameworks
- Geopolitical tensions (especially India-Pakistan) prevented effectiveness
- Economic imbalances and structural weaknesses hindered progress
- For meaningful SAARC: requires restructuring decision-making processes
- Efforts to depoliticize regional cooperation
- Allow economic and developmental agendas to take precedence
- Must prioritize over bilateral conflicts

## Q12. Give reasons for regionalization of world politics. (2013) (15 Marks)

### ğŸ¯ Core Theme

### ğŸ“ Answer Structure

**Introduction:**
- Regionalization gaining prominence
- Countries seek to address challenges collectively within regions
- Economic, security, political challenges
- Driven by several key factors
- Economic integration, security concerns, global institutions limitations
- Cultural affinities, smaller states' influence needs

**Reason 1 - Economic Integration:**
- Significant reason for regionalization
- Countries form economic blocs: EU, ASEAN, Mercosur
- Enhance trade, investment, development
- Regional organizations facilitate free trade agreements
- Common markets and economic cooperation
- Help members become more competitive globally
- Jagdish Bhagwati: regional trade agreements (RTAs) offer practical benefits
- Global trade negotiations under WTO can be slow and complex
- EU's success with single market and currency union
- Illustrates economic advantages of regional cooperation

**Reason 2 - Security Concerns:**
- Another major driver
- Countries collaborate regionally against threats
- Terrorism, organized crime, regional conflicts
- NATO: military alliance ensuring collective defense
- African Union (AU): developed peacekeeping capabilities
- Manages conflicts in Africa
- Barry Buzan: regional organizations respond more effectively to localized security
- Than global bodies hampered by geopolitical rivalries
- Slow decision-making processes

**Reason 3 - Limitations of Global Governance:**
- UN and global institutions promote regionalization
- UN's structural constraints: veto power in Security Council
- Often ineffective in addressing conflicts swiftly
- Countries turn to regional platforms for pragmatic solutions
- Regional bodies understand local dynamics better
- Can act faster
- Fill gaps left by global institutions

**Reason 4 - Cultural and Historical Affinities:**
- Further support regionalization
- Shared cultural, linguistic, or historical ties facilitate cooperation
- Arab League: common religion and culture
- Organization of Islamic Cooperation (OIC): religious unity
- Latin American countries: shared colonial histories
- Formed organizations like Mercosur to foster cooperation

**Reason 5 - Amplifying Influence for Smaller States:**
- Regionalization helps smaller/developing countries amplify influence
- By forming blocs, nations gain leverage
- Negotiations with global powers
- ASEAN negotiated effectively with U.S., China, EU
- Shows how regional cooperation increases strategic bargaining power
- Economic bargaining power enhanced

**Conclusion:**
- Regionalization driven by economic, security, political motivations
- Alongside cultural ties and strategic needs
- Offers pragmatic approach
- Countries collectively address challenges
- Amplify influence in interconnected world

- Accounting for 15% of global GDP
- Illustrates economic power of regional cooperation
- Introduction of euro in 1999
- Created common currency facilitating easier trade
- Reduces transaction costs for businesses
- Enhances economic stability

**Success 2 - Political Integration:**
- EU advanced political integration
- Through institutions: European Parliament, Commission, Council
- Created supranational structure
- Member states share sovereignty in certain policy areas
- Foreign affairs, security, environmental regulation
- Enabled coordinated responses to global challenges
- Climate change, terrorism
- EU's Common Foreign and Security Policy (CFSP)
- Allows presenting unified stance on international issues
- Amplifies influence in global diplomacy

**Success 3 - Peace and Stability:**
- EU's success in maintaining peace and stability
- Cannot be understated
- Preventing conflicts among historically antagonistic nations
- France and Germany
- Through economic interdependence and political cooperation
- Region once plagued by wars
- Now characterized by collaboration and mutual respect
- Nobel Peace Prize awarded to EU in 2012
- Recognized contributions to peace and reconciliation

**Success 4 - Global Norm-Setting:**
- EU as global norm-setter
- Trade agreements with Japan (EU-Japan EPA)
- Canada (CETA)
- Showcase ability to shape global economic standards
- Regulations on data protection (GDPR)
- Environmental policies
- Influenced practices worldwide
- Demonstrating EU's soft power

**Challenge 1 - Brexit:**
- Recent challenges threaten EU's cohesion and future
- Brexit: UK's withdrawal (2020)
- Highlighted tensions between national sovereignty and supranational governance
- Raised questions about EU's appeal and sustainability
- Emboldened Eurosceptic movements in other countries
- France, Italy, Netherlands
- Questioning benefits of EU membership

**Challenge 2 - Rising Nationalism and Populism:**
- Rising nationalism and populism across Europe
- Governments in Hungary and Poland
- Challenged EU's values and authority
- Disputes over rule of law, judicial independence, media freedom
- Created friction within union
- Raised concerns about EU's ability to enforce standards
- Uphold democratic principles among member states

**Challenge 3 - Migration Crisis:**
- Migration crisis: significant challenge
- Influx of refugees and migrants from Middle East and Africa
- Exposed divisions within EU
- Countries like Germany and Sweden: more welcoming approach
- Eastern European countries (Hungary, Poland): resistant
- Lack of unified migration policy strained solidarity
- Tested EU's commitment to human rights and humanitarian values

**Challenge 4 - Economic Disparities:**
- Economic disparities among member states
- Particularly evident during Greek debt crisis
- Exposed weaknesses in eurozone's economic governance
- Wealthier Northern countries vs struggling Southern economies
- Created tensions over fiscal policies and bailout measures
- Raising questions about sustainability of common currency
- Without deeper fiscal integration

**Challenge 5 - Democratic Deficit:**
- Criticisms of EU's "democratic deficit"
- Perception that decision-making processes
- Removed from ordinary citizens
- Dominated by technocrats and elites
- Led to disillusionment with EU project
- Contributed to rise of anti-EU sentiment

**Conclusion:**
- EU's achievements in economic integration, political cooperation, peace
- Make it most successful regional integration experiment
- However, faces significant challenges
- Brexit, nationalism, migration, economic disparities, democratic concerns
- EU's ability to address challenges
- While maintaining integration and respecting member state sovereignty
- Will determine its future trajectory
- Role as model for regional cooperation globally
